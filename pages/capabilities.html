FAQ

Scope clarity

What is a CMS?

Content Management System — the editing interface used to update site content after launch. We set up the CMS so your team can make changes without developer help.

What does responsive mean?

The site works well on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. Content reflows, touch targets adjust, and navigation adapts to each screen size.

Is bilingual included?

Bilingual-ready planning and bilingual-aware structure are part of our methodology where relevant. However, full bilingual delivery (complete English/Spanish content parity) is quoted separately unless expressly included in a specific package.

When should we start with Discovery?

Start with Discovery when you have unclear scope, multiple decision-makers, unclear platform/tool needs, messy legacy situations, or higher-risk approvals. Discovery protects against rework and mis-scoping by defining requirements before build commitment.

What is quoted separately?

Bilingual delivery, copywriting, migrations, ecommerce, advanced analytics, CRM automation, booking systems, calculators, custom integrations, rush delivery, and legacy cleanup are all quoted separately based on project-specific requirements.

What happens after launch?

Launch is not the end. Websites and systems require updates, refinements, and controlled iteration. Support Plans provide post-launch stability, growth enhancements, or embedded ongoing support—depending on your need state. Minimum 6-month commitment.

When does a website project become a systems project?

A website project addresses visibility and conversion: credibility, messaging, content structure. When the problem involves repeated manual workflows, scattered information, duplicated work, or operational friction that affects growth—this crosses into workflow/systems territory. Use the Website vs Systems guide in this page to identify your level.

Ready to start?
